How It Works

This tool overlays the business hours of up to four cities on a single 24-hour timeline — entirely inside your browser. No data is sent to a server.

Reading the Timeline

Each row represents one city. The 24 columns represent the UTC hours of the day (0 – 23). A cell is highlighted in teal when that UTC hour falls within 9 AM – 5 PM local time for that city. Hovering over any cell shows the exact local clock time and whether it is within business hours.

The Overlap strip below the city rows shows the degree of simultaneous coverage:

  •  Green — every selected city is in business hours — the ideal meeting slot.
  •  Orange — at least half of the cities are in business hours.
  •  Yellow — fewer than half of the cities are in business hours.
  •  Dark — no city is in business hours.

The column matching the current UTC hour is highlighted with a white outline and its label is shown in teal.

Best Meeting Windows

The Best Meeting Windows section lists every contiguous block of UTC hours where all selected cities are simultaneously within business hours (9 AM – 5 PM), together with the equivalent local-time range for each city. If no full overlap exists, the section falls back to the largest partial overlap.

Implementation Details

All time-zone conversions use the browser’s built-in Intl.DateTimeFormat API. For each UTC hour (0 – 23) the tool queries the local clock hour in every selected time zone using today’s date, so daylight saving time offsets are automatically taken into account. No third-party date library is required.

The timeline refreshes every minute so the current-hour indicator stays accurate without a page reload.

Privacy

All calculations run locally in your browser tab. No city selections or times are transmitted anywhere.
