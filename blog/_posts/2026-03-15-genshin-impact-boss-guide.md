Whether you are grinding ascension materials or pushing through the Spiral Abyss, understanding Genshin Impact's boss roster is essential. This guide covers every Normal Boss and Weekly Boss available up to **version 6.4**, with recommended elements and the one mechanic you absolutely must know for each fight.

---

## 🗺️ Normal Bosses

Normal Bosses cost **40 Original Resin** to claim rewards and respawn after roughly three minutes. They are the primary source of character ascension materials.

The table below is organised by region in the order they become available.

| # | Boss | Region | Recommended Elements / Characters | Critical Mechanic |
|---|------|--------|------------------------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Anemo Hypostasis** | Mondstadt | Electro · Pyro · Cryo | When the boss enters recovery, it summons Anemo pillars that heal it — destroy them all before they finish channelling. Anemo abilities deal almost no damage. |
| 2 | **Electro Hypostasis** | Mondstadt | Pyro · Cryo | At low HP it spawns three Electro prisms; you have a very short window to shatter them before it fully revives. Bring strong off-field DPS. |
| 3 | **Pyro Regisvine** | Liyue | Hydro · Cryo | Hit the glowing corolla (head) and the root (base) to stagger and open a damage window. Avoid Pyro characters entirely. |
| 4 | **Eye of the Storm** | Mondstadt | Cryo · Electro | Apply Cryo or Electro to ground it — it is airborne most of the fight and almost impossible to hit otherwise. |
| 5 | **Geo Hypostasis** | Liyue | Geo · Any strong DPS | Destroy its three stone pillars quickly during the revival phase. Bring a Geo character — their abilities can shatter the pillars in one hit. |
| 6 | **Oceanid / Hydro Tulpa** | Liyue (Qingce Village) | Pyro · Electro · Dendro | **Do not bring Cryo** — it heals the boss. For Hydro Tulpa (post-4.1), pop each tentacle cluster with Vaporise or Electro-Charged reactions; Dendro also triggers Bloom for burst damage. |
| 7 | **Primo Geovishap** | Liyue | Hydro (+ shield, off-element) | It periodically infuses itself with a random element and gains massive resistance to it. Bring a Hydro character to trigger Vaporise / counter Pyro infusion, and a reliable shield to survive its dive-slam. |
| 8 | **Cryo Regisvine** | Dragonspine | Pyro | Same two-weak-point pattern as the Pyro version. Avoid Cryo characters. The Dragonspine cold debuff (Sheer Cold) will drain your HP — cook warming food before the fight. |
| 9 | **Cryo Hypostasis** | Mondstadt (Dragonspine) | Pyro | During recovery it spawns Cryo crystals; use Pyro to melt them instantly. Its spinning slash is its biggest damage spike — time your dodge to the moment the ring completes. |
| 10 | **Ruin Serpent** | The Chasm | Geo · Burst DPS | Charge your **Lumenstone Adjuvant** by absorbing glowing rocks, then activate it to interrupt the boss's underground drill phase. Without this gadget, the fight is nearly impossible. |
| 11 | **Perpetual Mechanical Array** | Inazuma | Electro or Multi-element AoE | When the Array splits into satellite units, focus them to expose the core — the core is the only vulnerable state. Electro applies fastest. |
| 12 | **Thunder Manifestation** | Inazuma | Cryo · Non-Electro DPS | **Avoid Electro characters** — it heals from Electro damage. It creates a lightning cage that rapidly drains HP; sprint through one of the cage corners to escape. |
| 13 | **Maguu Kenki** | Inazuma | High single-target burst DPS | Phases through Anemo and Cryo stances; in the second phase it summons a spectral copy. Focus the original — the copy disappears when the main body dies. |
| 14 | **Pyro Hypostasis** | Inazuma | Hydro · Cryo | The dangerous moment is the phoenix flight phase: use **Hydro** to douse the flaming phoenix and drag it back to the ground. Without dousing it, the fight is extremely difficult. |
| 15 | **Hydro Hypostasis** | Inazuma | Pyro | During recovery it spawns multiple Hydro droplets. Pop as many as possible with Pyro (Vaporise) before they merge back into the boss. |
| 16 | **Golden Wolflord** | Inazuma | Geo (mandatory) | The Rift Hound skulls it summons at certain HP thresholds can only be destroyed by **Geo attacks**. Until they are destroyed, the boss is invulnerable. Bring at least one Geo character. |
| 17 | **Coral Defenders** | Inazuma | Pyro + Cryo (both on one team) | Two bosses — Wavechaser Beidou has a Cryo shield; Tidecaller Xu has a Pyro shield. You need both elements simultaneously to prevent them from reviving each other. |
| 18 | **Jadeplume Terrorshroom** | Sumeru | Pyro · Electro | When fully "Activated" (feathers glowing), the boss deals massive AoE damage. Use Pyro or Electro repeatedly to peel off feather stacks and force a stunned "Dormant" state for free DPS. |
| 19 | **Aeonblight Drake** | Sumeru | Electro (armour break) + Pyro/Cryo DPS | Shoot the glowing Electro nodes on each wing segment to disable the armour. All nodes must be hit in one cycle or it resets. Once armour is down, target the exposed core. |
| 20 | **Algorithm of Semi-Intransient Matrix of Overseen Network** | Sumeru | Multi-element (Pyro · Cryo · Electro) | Three distinct forms — each uses a different element. You **must** switch your attack element per phase to break its shield efficiently; staying monoelement makes the fight painfully long. |
| 21 | **Electro Regisvine** | Sumeru | Pyro · Cryo | Same weak-point mechanic as other Regisvines but with chain-lightning attacks. Cleanse Electro Elemental Infusion from your characters if using a Physical DPS. |
| 22 | **Setekh Wenut** | Sumeru (Desert of Hadramaveth) | Anemo · High burst DPS | During its wind-up phase, use **Anemo** on the Pollen Pods it spawns to trigger a Stormeye stagger. Without Anemo, it stays airborne and you cannot deal meaningful damage. |
| 23 | **Iniquitous Baptist** | Sumeru | Pyro + Cryo + Electro (all three required) | It cycles through three elemental shields (Pyro, Cryo, Electro). Each shield can only be broken by the matching counter-element. **You must bring all three** in one team — otherwise it regenerates shields indefinitely. |
| 24 | **Experimental Field Generator** | Sumeru (Devantaka Mountain) | Flexible — mirror the active element | It fires elemental orbs; absorbing the correct orb type into your characters staggers it. Adapt your reaction rotation to its current colour cycle. |
| 25 | **Hydro Tulpa** | Fontaine (replaces Oceanid) | Pyro · Electro · Dendro | See entry #6. **Do not bring Cryo** — Cryo heals the Hydro Armor on each limb. Stack Pyro/Electro for Vaporise/Overloaded reactions on each tentacle. |
| 26 | **Icewind Suite** (Dirge of Coppelia + Dirge of Brass) | Fontaine | Pyro + Cryo (both on one team) | Two automatons — one is Cryo, one is Pyro. They buff each other based on elemental proximity. You **must bring both Pyro and Cryo** to efficiently break each one's infusion shield and DPS both. |
| 27 | **Millennial Pearl Seahorse** | Fontaine | Electro · Pyro | Destroy the Hydro bubbles it uses as a shield before they trigger a full-HP Hydro burst. Target the glowing pearl on its forehead when it surfaces. |
| 28 | **Breacher Primus** | Fontaine | Electro (disarm) + any DPS | Hit the glowing joints on its arms with Electro to temporarily disable its armour. Without disarming it, its high Res values make the fight extremely slow. |
| 29 | **Solitary Suanni** | Natlan | Pyro (Nightsoul characters preferred) | It periodically enters an armoured state; absorbing the Pyro Phlogiston orbs it drops breaks the armour. Nightsoul-Blessing characters from Natlan gain bonus effects in this fight. |
| 30 | **Emperor of Fire and Iron** | Natlan | Hydro · Cryo | An aggressively mobile boss with multi-hit combo strings. Break its Pyro armour with Hydro (Vaporise) or Cryo (Melt) first; otherwise it regenerates HP rapidly. Dodge its ground-slam shockwaves by jumping. |
| 31 | **Legatus Golem** | Natlan | Nightsoul-Blessing (Pyro) · Strong burst DPS | Activate the Natlan Ley Lines by using Nightsoul-Blessing characters near the arena totems. The Phlogiston charge significantly weakens its Phlogiston Armor. Without this mechanic, the golem is nearly unkillable. |
| 32 | **Statue of Marble and Brass** | Natlan | Pyro · Geo | Alternates between a Marble phase (Geo shield, break with Pyro) and a Brass phase (Pyro infusion, break with Hydro/Cryo). Matching attack element to the wrong phase deals almost zero damage. |
| 33 | **Tenebrous Papilla** | Natlan | Pyro + Dendro/Electro | Its Shadow Armour can only be dispelled by spending Nightsoul Blessing charges from Natlan characters near it. Burning or Quicken reactions speed up the armour-stripping. Do not let it enter its full-shadow Enraged state. |
| 34 | **Frost Sovereign** | Snezhnaya | Pyro · Electro | Brings Cryo infusion to the field that gradually freezes the arena floor, slowing characters. Focus the ice-crystal anchors scattered around the arena to remove the slowing ice. Avoid Cryo and Hydro characters (freeze locks you). |
| 35 | **Fatui Harbinger Construct** | Snezhnaya | Electro · Pyro | A mechanical construct using Cryo-Electro hybrid attacks. Interrupt its charging phase by targeting the exposed Delusion Core; failing to interrupt triggers a full-arena wipe AOE. |
| 36 | **Glacial Throne Warden** | Snezhnaya | Pyro · Dendro · Anemo | Three-phase fight centred on temperature management. Watch the frost meter at the top of the screen — it rises with every Cryo hit from the boss and resets only when you deal Pyro reactions. Anemo Swirl disperses frozen projectiles. |

## 👑 Weekly Bosses

Weekly Bosses live in **Trounce Domains** and cost **30 Original Resin** for the first three clears each week (60 Resin thereafter). They drop talent ascension materials and boss-exclusive items. The fights are notably harder and often have mandatory mechanics.

| # | Boss | Domain / Location | Recommended Elements / Characters | Critical Mechanic |
|---|------|-------------------|------------------------------------|-|
| 1 | **Stormterror Dvalin** | Stormterror's Lair, Mondstadt | Cryo · Pyro · Physical | Dvalin perches and exposes glowing crystal clusters on his neck and claws. **You must destroy these crystals** before attacking his head or the damage window closes. Bring a food buff — healing items from Mondstadt can be used mid-fight. |
| 2 | **Lupus Boreas (Andrius, Wolf of the North)** | Wolvendom, Mondstadt | Pyro · Electro (no Cryo / Anemo) | **You cannot heal in this fight**. Andrius is highly resistant to Cryo and Anemo — avoid those elements. Focus on clean rotations and keeping characters alive; bring shielders like Zhongli or Layla. |
| 3 | **Childe (Tartaglia)** | Golden House, Liyue | Electro · Cryo | Three phases: Hydro ranged → melee (most dangerous) → Hydro AoE emergency. In the melee phase he dashes and deals burst combos — dodge the Riptide cross-slash. Burst him hard in Phase 1 to shorten Phase 2. |
| 4 | **Azhdaha** | Beneath the Dragon-Queller, Liyue | Varied (opposite of infused element) | Azhdaha infuses one or two elements at random each week (Pyro, Cryo, Electro, or Hydro). Check the domain description for his current element and bring the counter. His arena-wide roar locks you in place — activate a shield *before* entering the fight. |
| 5 | **La Signora** | Tenshukaku, Inazuma | Hydro (Phase 1) / Cryo (Phase 2) | Phase 1 (Cryo): bring Pyro to melt her cryo cage fast. Phase 2 (Pyro): bring Hydro to vaporise her fire attacks. A **temperature gauge** fills as she uses elemental attacks — if it maxes out, your characters take massive damage; keep swapping between elements to suppress it. |
| 6 | **Raiden Shogun (Plane of Euthymia)** | End of the Oneiric Euthymia, Inazuma | Electro (yes, Electro is fine here) · Any strong DPS | In Phase 2 the Shogun enters **Baleful Shadowlord** mode with a near-impenetrable shield. The shield depletes based on **Elemental Burst damage** from your characters — use every Elemental Burst you have the moment she raises the shield. Keep Burst-ready characters in rotation. |
| 7 | **Everlasting Lord of Arcane Wisdom (Shouki no Kami)** | Joururi Workshop, Sumeru | Electro · Anemo · Hydro | The Mechanical Puppet fires Balethunder projectiles that inflict stacking Electrogranum debuffs. Dodge or Swirl them with Anemo to cleanse. Its **energy cannon charge** is telegraphed — dash through the beam path to avoid oneshot. Breaking the chest core with concentrated Electro damage speeds up Phase 2 significantly. |
| 8 | **All-Devouring Narwhal** | Foul Legacy's Lair, Fontaine | Pyro · Electro · Cryo | The Narwhal periodically becomes **invulnerable and dives** — during the dive, attack the glowing bubbles on its tail to cancel the dive early and deal bonus damage. It also leaves behind trailing water jets; run perpendicular to them rather than straight back. |
| 9 | **The Knave (Arlecchino)** | Masquerade of the Guilty, Fontaine | Hydro · Cryo | She places a **Blood-Debt Directive** debuff on characters; if you allow three stacks to expire without dealing Pyro damage to her, she heals for a large portion of her max HP. Always maintain a Pyro applicator on field. Her Spear Rain phase is heavily telegraphed — dodge sideways, not backwards. |
| 10 | **The Wayob Manifestation (Ocelotl)** | Cradle of Thunder, Natlan | Pyro · Hydro · Dendro | The boss channels a totem that empowers it with Nightsoul energy. **Destroy the three Nightsoul Totems** in the arena quickly; failing to do so triggers a large AoE resonance blast. Nightsoul-Blessing characters from Natlan can destroy totems in a single hit. |
| 11 | **Iktomi (Capitano, The Captain)** | Fields of Vindagnyr, Natlan | Anemo · Pyro · Physical | A Pyro/Physical hybrid boss with a unique **Phlogiston Gauge** mechanic. When the gauge fills (due to Pyro hits from him), he enters Berserker mode with vastly increased attack speed. Use Anemo Swirl to disperse Pyro auras and keep the gauge low. Natlan characters reduce the gauge passively with Nightsoul Blessing. |
| 12 | **Tsaritsa's Vanguard (The Eleventh Harbinger)** | Frost Palace, Snezhnaya | Pyro · Electro | Opens with a **Cryo barrier dome** that prevents leaving the arena and deals periodic Cryo damage. The dome shrinks every 20 seconds; destroy the Cryo anchors (4 total) to dismiss it. Failure to do so leads to a rapid freeze-to-death scenario. Inside the dome, Hydro is a liability — it freezes your characters on contact with Cryo surfaces. |
| 13 | **Tsaritsa (The Cryo Archon)** | Heart of Eternal Frost, Snezhnaya | Pyro · Pyro · Pyro | A three-phase archon fight. Phase 1: destroy five Cryo pillars to shatter her ice throne and force her down. Phase 2: she cycles through all Cryo abilities — maintain a permanent Pyro aura to prevent freeze. Phase 3: a **100-second countdown** DPS check; she becomes invincible unless you hit her "Gnosis Core" (exposed only during her charged attack animation). Bring your highest-damage Pyro characters and a reliable Pyro battery. |

## 🌟 General Tips

<div class="alert alert-info">
  💡 <b>Bring a shielder everywhere.</b> Characters like Zhongli, Layla, or Noelle dramatically reduce the margin for error against multi-hit boss combos, especially on weekly bosses.
</div>

- **Check element resistances first** — bosses like Lupus Boreas and Thunder Manifestation actively punish you for using their own element. One quick look at the domain description saves a painful run.
- **Cooldown management matters** — weekly bosses are DPS checks. Align your Elemental Bursts so they fire off during windows when the boss is staggered or stunned.
- **Don't ignore the environment** — Ruin Serpent requires the Lumenstone Adjuvant gadget; Setekh Wenut requires Anemo on Pollen Pods; Golden Wolflord requires Geo. Some fights are literally impossible without the correct tool or element.
- **Food buffs are your friend** — always cook attack-boosting or stamina-replenishing dishes before weekly bosses. They are cheap, refillable, and can be the difference in a close run.
- **Weekly Boss reset is Monday 4:00 AM server time** — plan your resin accordingly to hit all three discounted clears before the week ends.
